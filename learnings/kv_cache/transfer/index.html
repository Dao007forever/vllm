KV Cache Transfer for P/D Disaggregation

How vLLM ships KV cache between a prefill worker (P) and a decode worker (D) so they can be scaled, profiled, and hardware-targeted independently. Covers the NIXL connector, both Mooncake connectors, and how each survives heterogeneous TP/PP/DP between P and D.

What's here

Five notes covering the transfer plane sitting between prefill and decode in a disaggregated vLLM deployment. We treat the KV cache as a shared resource that must be moved — not just allocated — and we look at the three transports vLLM ships in tree.

New here? Start with PD_DISAGG.html for the protocol contract every connector implements. Then read the transport you care about. PARALLELISM.html is the cross-cutting note explaining how heterogeneous TP/PP between P and D is reconciled.

All notes

Protocol & survey

  • PD_DISAGG.html Why split prefill and decode in the first place. The five-method KVConnectorBase_V1 contract every transport implements. Push vs pull as a design choice. Where the cache transfer plane sits relative to vLLM's scheduler and worker.
  • PARALLELISM.html How NIXL, the direct Mooncake pipe, and the Mooncake store each handle TPP ≠ TPD. Head-dimension splitting, GQA dedup, MLA replication, Mamba's three-projection conv state. Why PP rarely differs in practice but DP routinely does.
  • ABSTRACTIONS.html The mathematical objects under heterogeneous-parallel transfer: the KV cache as a product index space, sharding as a partition, the transfer plan as a common refinement (meet) that factorizes per axis — P∧D = (PL∧DL)×(PH∧DH). Maps to DTensor / GSPMD / CuTe and sketches an Axis-based planner. Companion theory note to PARALLELISM.

Transports

  • NIXL.html D-initiated READ (pull) over NIXL agents. ZMQ side-channel handshake + lazy per-engine agent registration. All-at-once batched async transfer, polled in the scheduler loop. Lease-with-heartbeat keeps P from freeing blocks too early.
  • MOONCAKE_PIPE.html P-initiated PUSH via batch_transfer_sync_write. FastAPI bootstrap server publishes worker addresses; D queries, sends a request with its own block addresses, P sends KV directly into them. The direct Mooncake connector — no shared store.
  • MOONCAKE_STORE.html Producer batch_puts, consumer batch_gets. Both go through a distributed KV store keyed by content hash. Decouples P and D in time (cache hits across engine runs), at the cost of one extra hop and a coordinator. Companion read: connector/MOONCAKE.md.
